THE 60-SECOND POSITION

In 1914, a law-abiding Briton could live, work, and travel without the state asking permission or checking their papers. That world is gone.

Today, the British state records your speech as intelligence even when no crime has occurred. It scans your bank accounts without individual suspicion. It can seize money from your account without a court order. It plans to make it illegal to work without a government-issued digital identity. And it is building AI-powered surveillance systems that can track every face, every transaction, and every movement in real time.

Each of these powers was introduced as a targeted, proportionate response to a specific problem: fraud, terrorism, illegal immigration. But the pattern is always the same. What starts as exceptional becomes ordinary. What targets the few expands to monitor the many. The presumption of innocence is quietly replaced by the presumption of suspicion.

The UK has no constitutional guarantee of free speech, no structural limit on surveillance, and no hard legal firewall between the citizen and the state’s expanding digital reach. The infrastructure for total control is being assembled in plain sight — not by tyrants, but by ordinary politicians responding to ordinary incentives.

Fighting for a Free Future exists because these freedoms will not defend themselves. The time to draw hard lines is before they are crossed — not after.

Key Evidence

  1. Over 12,000 arrests for offensive online messages in 2023 alone — a 58% increase since before the pandemic. There is not and has never really been such a thing as freedom of speech in UK law.
    Britain’s Free Speech Frankenstein — Liam Noble Shearer
  2. Labour’s Eligibility Verification Notices compel banks to run mass automated checks on millions of accounts without individual suspicion, replacing the presumption of innocence with population-wide financial surveillance.
    Tyranny of the Month: ‘Bank Spying’ Powers — Steve Baker
  3. Since 2015, HMRC can instruct banks to transfer money directly from accounts without a court order, for debts over £1,000. The citizen must seek judicial oversight after the fact.
    Tyranny of the Month: HMRC Powers — Steve Baker
  4. Mandatory digital ID by 2029 reverses the presumption of liberty: instead of the state justifying intrusion, citizens must prove permission to exist, work, and transact.
    Tyranny of the Month: Digital ID — Steve Baker
